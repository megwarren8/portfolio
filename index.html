Probability & Matrices

Course textbook · 25 sections · grades 10 to 12 · one HTML file

Every choice in this book is backed by something, not a guess. Before writing a single section, I pulled research on what actually keeps teenagers engaged with material like this. I scraped it, then distilled it into patterns, the dos and don'ts that guide how the book reads, not just what it teaches. When I do not know something for certain, I look it up. I would rather spend an hour confirming a fact than ship a guess.

The same engine behind this book, one source that forks into a student edition and a facilitator's answer-key edition, works just as well for a corporate onboarding guide or a certification study guide. That is a commission I take on just as readily as a classroom textbook.

Grouper

Classroom tool · in daily use · one HTML file

Built to run 3 Minds, 1 Marker, the daily random-grouping routine from Building Thinking Classrooms. Save your classes and rosters once and they're just there every day, no re-typing a list. Every group gets real roles (Scribe, Speaker, Inquirer), and a built-in timer keeps rounds moving. A separate presentation screen shows the room a clean projected view instead of the teacher's own working screen. Groups are genuinely random, and the tool remembers your last several rounds, so it avoids repeating a pairing from recent memory. No student is ever left alone: when the numbers don't divide evenly, the tool builds one group of three instead of leaving someone out.

Wildcard

Icebreaker · 190 questions · one HTML file

I built it to open class every so often. Real celebrity-interview questions land better with students than the usual icebreaker-deck fluff. So I scraped good ones from podcast transcripts, then rewrote the best so whoever spins can actually answer, not just the specific celebrity who was originally asked. Filter by podcast or by vibe, and pick more than one of each.

Color Companion

Color matcher · full marker set · one HTML file

Built for myself first. I hated being lied to by the plastic caps on even high-end art supplies, since the cap color and the real ink are rarely the same. So I swatched every marker I own and built a matcher instead. Upload a photo of anything you want to recreate, or snap a printed swatch sheet, and it tells you exactly which of your own markers to reach for. No more guessing.

Ask the Turtle

Marketing site + free tools · for a Boston consultancy

The live marketing website for a nonprofit-fundraising consultancy, built end to end. Navy is the anchor (used instead of black). Lime is the single accent, one pop per view. The listening rings, three concentric ripples in lime, sky, and teal, literalize the brand's promise to listen first. I took the founder's vision and built the site that carries it.

My favorite page on the site was an idea I had partway through the build, an add-on I was fully prepared to cut when we reviewed the final draft together. It stayed: a free Tools page with working fundraising calculators (a gift range chart, an ask-amount suggester, a retention and ROI tool) that visitors can use before ever hiring the firm. The kind of thing big firms keep behind a login, given away as the handshake.

Selected private work

Prospect Waterfall

Nonprofit research tool · built for one client · not public

A waterfall-enrichment engine for nonprofit development work. Give it an organization's name, and it pulls real IRS Form 990 financial data, computes an actual multi-year funding trend instead of a single snapshot, and finds and verifies real contacts there. Each contact is checked by a real evaluator before it counts as a hit, and every provider it tries is logged, so nothing is a guess dressed up as a result. Built for a fundraising consultancy's internal use, and it stays that way: the exact discovery logic is part of what makes it valuable to the people who paid for it.

Daily Lesson Kit

Teaching companion · Trig, Derivatives, Integrals

The most ambitious thing I've built for my own classroom: a daily companion that runs the actual routines research says work. It bundles a silent-scribe warm-up, an honest check-in, and Grouper, all built right in. Live lunch and advisory feed data keeps it actually current, not a static template. Every research-grounded habit in my teaching lives in one place here. It stays private because it's tuned to my own courses and curriculum, but a version built around your own daily routines is exactly the kind of thing I take on commission.
